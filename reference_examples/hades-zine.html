VOLUME TWO ¬∑ HADES & APHRODITE
Before the first flower withered, before the first soul learned the way to the silent meadows, the Underworld was merely a hollow echo.
Hades sat upon a throne of cold obsidian, his heart a clock of ticking stone. He was the inevitability of the end, a god who counted the cost of every life in shadows and silence.
He believed he was complete in his solitude‚Äîuntil the surface world bled into his own.
The Styx curled against black shores like a dying serpent. Asphodel stretched grey and endless, dust upon dust, memory upon fading memory.
Iron. That's what he was made of. Unyielding, unmoved, the final equation. No warmth. No wanting. Only the quiet mathematics of ending.
Aphrodite did not fall into the dark; she invaded it.
She was the salt-sting of the sea and the fever of a summer noon, wreathed in a beauty so sharp it cut through the gloom like a blade.
Where she stepped on the dry, grey earth of Asphodel, the dust grew heavy with the scent of crushed jasmine.
She was the pulse of the beginning, the chaotic hunger that drives a seed to break through soil. The ache in the throat before the first cry. The wanting that births stars.
Rose. Living, breathing, blooming even in the dark.
They met where the Styx curdles against the shore. He, a monolith of shadowed iron; she, a riot of shimmering foam and gold.
"You have no business here," Hades spoke, his voice the sound of grinding tectonic plates.
"Nothing grows in this soil. Nothing breathes. There is no room for your restless wanting."
His words were fortress walls. His presence, the weight of finality. He was the punctuation mark at the end of every story.
She should have fled.
Aphrodite stepped closer, the hem of her gossamer robes catching on jagged basalt.
She did not flinch at the cold.
"You are the only thing in this universe that does not move for me," she whispered, her eyes bright with a dangerous curiosity.
"The gods flee from the end, but you are the end. I wanted to see if the heart of the world still beat in the dark."
She was the only force that ever dared question his dominion. Not with rebellion, but with wonder.
