The Shadow in the Green
Robin was the echo of Prometheus‚Äîthe clever mind turned to the service of the small. He was a man of the edge, a ghost in the thicket who had learned that a law is only as strong as the fear of the man who wrote it.
He lived in the "between," a shadow with a bow of yew, stealing the sunlight of the rich to warm the hearths of the shivering. But even a ghost can grow thin; even a rebel can lose himself in the cold geometry of survival.
He was knowledge turned to steel, a map of the forest with no heart at the center.

Long after the gods had retreated into the marrow of the mountains, and the titans were nothing but the hum of the tectonic plates, the fire and the iron found a new home in the veins of the desperate.

The Fire Walks In
The stories say she was a lady of the court, a prize in a cage of silk. They lie.
Marion was the daughter of the first chaos, the same Fire that had once hidden in a fennel stalk, now wearing the skin of a woman who had realized that her privilege was a funeral shroud.
She did not wait to be rescued. She walked into the green-wood with a blade hidden in her bodice and a hunger for the wild that made the ancient oaks tremble.

They met at the crossing of a stream, neither willing to yield the path. Robin saw a target; Marion saw an obstacle. It was only when their steel clashed‚Äîthe ring of iron against iron‚Äîthat they recognized the old, cosmic friction.
"You are a thief of purses," she said, her eyes flashing like embers in a draft. "But you are still a prisoner to their rules. You steal their gold, but you still believe in their value. I came here to burn the ledger entirely."
Robin lowered his sword, the hollow place in his chest suddenly filled with a roar of heat. "The forest is a cold mother. It takes a certain kind of madness to survive her."
"I am not here to survive," she whispered, stepping into his guard. "I am here to thrive. I am the summer that breaks the frost."
